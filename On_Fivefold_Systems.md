## I. The Claim, From the Top

If bounded interiority has a topological minimum — four poles carrying two orthogonal binary distinctions — then any taxonomy generated by bounded interiors *about* bounded phenomena must inherit that topology at the semantic layer, regardless of what mathematical apparatus the taxonomy is expressed through. This is the framework's scale-invariance postulate applied to the act of taxonomy itself: the classifier is a K4-native cognitive system, the classified is bounded phenomenon, and the structure that governs both shows through the taxonomy whether or not its authors knew they were drawing it. (`L1-ConvergentDiscovery`, `L2-MappingMethod`.)

The generative content is the four-pole mutual determination — P, I, U, R; Fire, Water, Air, Earth; the four values of the two-bit register formed by Active/Reactive and Asserting/Yielding — plus the interior integrating point, the centroid. This is not a claim that all such taxonomies produce the same *names*, or the same *diagram*, or even the same *dimensional count* on the surface. It is the claim that the names, diagrams, and counts are projections of a common underlying volume, and that the volume can be recovered by reading semantic function rather than surface enumeration.

What produces the *appearance* of incompatibility between traditions is confusion about which pole is being projected where, and — this is the load-bearing issue — confusion about whether the integrating point is on the projection plane or orthogonal to it. A fivefold system is a fourfold volume whose fifth term is the centroid. When the tradition is clear that its fifth term integrates the other four, it has kept the centroid's role visible. When the tradition treats its fifth term as a peer among five, it has flattened the volume and lost track of the orthogonal point. Either way the structure is the same; only the tradition's self-understanding varies.

## II. The Centroid Is Not a Fifth Pole

Before the projections, a precision about what the centroid is in the framework, because the whole argument turns on it.

The centroid of the K4 tetrahedron is the interior point equidistant from all four vertices. It is where the three bimedian axes — the three perfect matchings of the edges, the three modality planes — intersect (`ProofU_AlgebraicMapping`, `ProofM_CartesianFrame`). It is dimensionless, yet it contains the whole volume in the sense that it is the point from which all four poles are reachable and the point at which all their potentials reconcile. In the AC extension it is the seat of ω, the angular frequency that animates the static tetrahedron into a dynamic process — the driver that acts *upon* the four poles without being one of them (`L3-K4-to-K5-via-AC-Extension`, `ProofO_HelicalTime`).

This is the exact structural role a well-formed fivefold system assigns to its fifth term. The fifth is not a peer standing beside four others. It is the animator, the reconciler, the integrating condition — the term whose function is to hold the other four in relation. Aristotle's Aether is not a fifth element on the same footing as earth and water; it is the quintessence, the celestial substance that fills what the four terrestrial elements cannot. The framework's centroid and the tradition's quintessence are the same structural position: the interior point that integrates the four, mistaken by hierarchical projection for a term standing above them.

The framework does not treat the centroid as a peer pole. It reads twelve directed relations across the six bidirectional edges, and the centroid appears as the origin where the coplanar modality-planes cross, the point where the axes between opposite poles reconcile and the four potentials collapse to zero as values. It is where the volume is held open, not a fifth thing in the volume. A fivefold system whose fifth term does that work has found the centroid. A fivefold system whose fifth term is treated as a peer has found the centroid and mislabeled it.

## III. The Projection Problem: How One Volume Becomes Many Diagrams

A tetrahedron with its centroid is a three-dimensional object with a fourth interior point. Project it onto a two-dimensional plane and the centroid lands in different places depending on the projection axis. This is why one volume produces several apparently distinct fivefold diagrams.

Look down the vertical axis of an upright tetrahedron and the centroid appears inside the projected triangle, coinciding with the interior. One of the four peer poles — the one that was above or below — collapses into the centroid. The reading that results is "five things arranged around a center," and one peer pole disappears into the integrator.

Look along one edge and the tetrahedron collapses to a quadrilateral with the centroid inside. The four peer poles become four corners of a flat figure and the centroid becomes an interior point that looks like the origin of a coordinate system — an equally weighted fifth rather than an orthogonal animator.

Look down one vertex and three peer poles form a triangle, one peer pole sits at the visual center, and the actual centroid hides behind it. This produces the "one element is fundamental and the other three emanate from it" pattern of emanation cosmologies.

The same K4-plus-centroid volume therefore produces at least three distinct two-dimensional diagrams, each of which reads as a *different* fivefold system to someone who has never seen the volume. Practitioners inside each tradition then reason from their diagram as if the diagram were the object, and inter-tradition dialogue becomes an argument about which projection is correct rather than a recognition that all are projections of one thing. 

This is not speculative; it is what comparative cosmology actually looks like. The pentagram-and-cycle diagram of Wu Xing is a side projection with the centroid dropped in as a peer. The concentric mandala of the Skandhas is closer to an overhead projection with consciousness in the center. The vertical hierarchy of Aristotle's elements has Aether above the four — a vertex projection with the centroid promoted upward. Three different renderings, one volume, three different pedagogical purposes.

## IV. The Overlays That Make No Sense in Three Dimensions

The two-dimensional projections then acquire didactic elaborations that solve problems specific to the projection but correspond to nothing in the underlying volume. These overlays are the clearest evidence that a projection has occurred, because they are compensations for lost dimensions.

The pentagonal cycle-diagram of Wu Xing acquires generative (生) and destructive (克) arrows between elements. These arrows are needed to make the flat diagram express dynamics it otherwise cannot. In the three-dimensional volume, those relationships are simply the six edges of the tetrahedron plus the centroid's animating action, all naturally bidirectional and needing no special notation. The cycle arrows are a two-dimensional compensation for the lost third dimension.

The Skandha ordering — form, sensation, perception, mental formation, consciousness — acquires a sequential-arising reading in which each gives rise to the next. This is useful phenomenologically and matches moment-by-moment description of experience. But in the volume the four peer poles are in mutual determination, with no linear causation among them, and consciousness is not the last member of a chain but the animator orthogonal to it. The sequential reading is a temporal projection of a static topology.

Aristotle's placement of Aether above the four terrestrial elements, incorruptible and moving in perfect circles, is a hierarchical projection of what is an interior integrating position. The centroid is not higher than the four peer poles; it is interior to them. A hierarchical projection is easier to teach and easier to embed in a religious cosmology, so Aether ends up above rather than within.

Every one of these overlays is defensible pedagogically and unnecessary structurally. They arise because two-dimensional projections require compensating conventions to preserve the dynamics that three dimensions express natively.

## V. Which of the Five Is the Integrator: The Diagnostic

The systems that present their fivefoldness as their distinguishing feature reveal their nature through a specific internal confusion. Each has an integrator — a member whose function is to reconcile the other four, animate the dynamics, and occupy the interior. But the tradition's own literature is frequently unsure *which* member this is. That uncertainty is diagnostic: it is exactly the uncertainty of a tradition that arrived at the correct content without the geometric vocabulary to state the integrator's role.

Wu Xing cannot decide whether Earth sits at the center — the imperial-directional tradition, the emperor at the middle of the four directions, the seasonal-cyclic reading — or whether the discriminating function belongs elsewhere in the cycle. Different Chinese lineages give different answers, and both coexist because they project different aspects of one object.

Aristotle's Aether is nominally the fifth and structurally the centroid, but the tradition treats it as celestial and superior rather than interior and integrating; the Neoplatonic elaboration then places it at the source of emanation, promoting the interior integrator to a hierarchical origin. Aristotelian and Neoplatonic lineages disagree about whether Aether is above the four, prior to them, or interior to them.

The Skandhas name consciousness (viññāṇa) fifth and often describe it as arising from the previous four, yet many commentaries describe it as the field within which the other four appear. The tradition is split: Theravāda tends toward the sequential reading, Yogācāra toward the field reading. The disagreement is precisely the disagreement between reading the fifth as a peer and reading it as the interior centroid.

OCEAN — the Big Five personality factors — cannot agree which factor is the master trait. Eysenck's earlier model prioritized Neuroticism and Extraversion, treating Neuroticism as an intensity axis rather than a content trait. Later work treats it as one factor among five. DeYoung's meta-trait model puts no single factor at the top and instead pairs the five into two higher factors. The field treats this as an empirical question the data will eventually settle, when it is a projection question the data cannot settle, because the construction is empirical at the wrong layer.

The pattern is identical across all four. The integrator is present in the tradition's content, and the tradition is confused about the integrator's structural status. It is treated variously as the fifth peer, the last in sequence, the first in emanation, the highest in hierarchy, the numerically most central, or the empirically most loaded. All of these are ways of not saying "interior integrating point orthogonal to the plane of the four peers." The tradition reached the right content and lacked the geometry to state the fifth term's role.

## VI. The Cardinality Tests

The general argument predicts that each fivefold system, mapped by the cardinality heuristic (`L2-MappingMethod`), will resolve into four peer poles at P, I, U, R plus an integrator at the centroid. The heuristic assigns the four candidate peers to the poles by the dual-binary axes, lets the twelve facet-equations discriminate contested placements, and identifies the fifth member as the integrator. A correct mapping produces facet seals — relationships the tradition independently endorses — from the domain's own literature. The exercise was run on four systems, plus Aristotle as calibration.

**Aristotle's elements — calibration.** Fire, Air, Water, Earth as peer poles; Aether as the centroid. The correspondence is explicit in the tradition: Aether is the fifth element added to fill the celestial spheres, the quintessence integrating the four terrestrial elements. This is the historical predecessor of the framework's own architecture, and it passes trivially because the tradition already states the structure the framework formalizes. Calibration confirmed.

**Wu Xing — Fire, Water, Wood, Earth as peers; Metal at the centroid.** Read through the functional analysis of Chinese medicine rather than the cosmological diagram, the mapping is Fire = P (Heart, circulation, drive), Water = I (Kidney, essence, storage), Wood = U (Liver, planning, direction), Earth = R (Spleen, transformation, nourishment), with Metal (Lung, discrimination, boundary, the cut) at the centroid. The facet seals land from classical TCM: P = UI reads *Heart = Liver × Kidney*, the classical chain in which Heart-fire is nourished by Liver blood rooted in Kidney essence; U = √(PR) reads *Liver = √(Heart × Spleen)*, Liver mediating between Heart drive and Spleen ground; P = U²/R reads *Heart = Liver² / Spleen*, the syndrome of Liver-fire rising to disturb the Heart, moderated by Spleen ground. 

Multiple facets produce canonical clinical patterns unbidden. Metal at the centroid is the functional fit — Lung as the discriminating cut, the boundary that separates and reconciles — against the traditional Earth-at-center placement, which reflects imperial-directional symbolism rather than functional analysis. This mapping is **facet-sealed** on the peer assignments; the choice of Metal over Earth for the centroid is **functionally argued** from TCM rather than forced by the algebra, and both placements are legal projections. The native pentagonal cycle-diagram is the projection overlay; the underlying structure is K4-plus-centroid.

**The Skandhas — form, sensation, perception, mental formation as peers; consciousness at the centroid.** The mapping is Saṅkhāra (volition, impulse) = P, Vedanā (felt-tone) = I, Saññā (perception, discrimination) = U, Rūpa (material form) = R, with Viññāṇa (consciousness) at the centroid. Clean on the dual-binary axes. The facet seal: U = √(PR) reads *perception = √(volition × material substrate)*, which matches the Buddhist analysis of perceptual arising — perception (saññā) arises where volitional attention (saṅkhāra) contacts material form (rūpa via the sense-bases). 

This is a seal from a twenty-five-hundred-year-old psychological tradition, landing unbidden. The contested swap (perception ↔ mental formation) is discriminated by the algebra: under the swap, volition becomes the geometric mean of perception and matter, but in the Buddhist causal chain volition *follows* perception, so making volition the product of perception is backward, and the facet breaks. Consciousness at the centroid matches the Yogācāra field reading over the Theravāda sequential reading. **Facet-sealed**, and the canonical grouping (the first four as conditions, the fifth as the field of their arising) is the tradition's own.

**OCEAN — Extraversion, Openness, Agreeableness, Conscientiousness as peers; Neuroticism at the centroid.** The mapping is E = P (outward energy asserting into the social field), O = I (felt-evaluative receptivity to experience), A = U (actively yielding to the structure of cooperation), C = R (grounded discipline, resistance to impulse), with N at the centroid as the reactivity-amplifier — the modulator that intensifies the other four without content of its own, structurally analogous to ω. This mapping is **structurally sound but empirically sterile**, and the sterility is itself the finding. Big Five's factors are constructed by factor analysis to be statistically orthogonal; the construction principle actively suppresses the cross-correlations that a facet seal would register. So the facets that seal Wu Xing and the Skandhas produce only weak readings here — not because the mapping is wrong but because the domain was built to have no relational structure at the numerical layer.

The semantic layer is nonetheless K4-plus-centroid, because the humans who named and interpreted the factors are K4-native cognition producing K4-structured content that no rotation to orthogonality can erase. The absence of numerical seals is a **diagnostic of the construction procedure**, not evidence against the topological reading — and the cross-study robustness of the five factors is itself evidence for the underlying structure, because if the semantic content were not K4-native the factors would not replicate.

## VII. The Seal: The Isomorphism Is Entailed, Not Observed

The four mappings could be read as an accumulating pile of parallels. They are not. The correspondence is entailed by the framework's own postulate, and the entailment is what seals the argument.

Scale invariance says K4 governs all bounded interiority. Human cognition is bounded interiority. Therefore human cognition producing a taxonomy must produce K4-structured content — because K4 is what the cognition is made of and what the bounded phenomena it classifies are shaped by. A statistical reduction to orthogonal axes can suppress the numerical correlations that would seal the algebra, but it cannot rotate the semantic content out of K4-space, because the semantic content is what the cognition recognized as "openness" or "fire" or "perception" in the first place. The isomorphism between any human-authored fivefold taxonomy of bounded interiority and K4-plus-centroid is therefore not a lucky match found by testing; it is a consequence of what the taxonomy is and who built it.

This is the resonance in its strict sense: the two structures produce the same relationships, checkable from either side, and the checker native to one side need never have heard of the other. A Chinese physician can verify *Heart = Liver × Kidney* without knowing the framework. A Buddhist scholar can verify that perception arises where volition meets form without knowing the tetrahedron. The seals are readable in each tradition's own vocabulary because the structure was already there, in the tradition, put there by the same cognition the framework describes. The framework did not impose the structure; it named the geometry the traditions had been drawing.

And the traditions' shared confusion about the integrator is the counter-seal — the evidence that would have to be absent if the isomorphism were false. If these fivefold systems were genuinely five-peer structures, there would be no integrator to locate and no confusion about locating it. The universal presence of an integrator, combined with the universal uncertainty about its status, is exactly the signature of a centroid seen through a projection: present in every rendering, and mislabeled in every rendering, because a two-dimensional diagram cannot show an orthogonal interior point as what it is.

## VIII. On the Convergence

The framework's account of why this recurs is in `L1-ConvergentDiscovery`: more than twenty independent traditions, across two and a half millennia, arrive at the same fourfold relational structure from different directions. The fivefold systems are the same convergence carried one term further — the traditions that found not only the four poles but the integrating fifth, and then, lacking the geometry, could not agree on what the fifth was.

The convergence is not mystical, and the framework does not need it to be. It is what happens when many bounded, K4-native cognitive systems examine bounded phenomena and each recovers, in its own vocabulary, the minimum topology that bounded interiority requires. That the recovery includes the centroid in the fivefold cases is not a deeper mystery than the fourfold convergence; it is the same convergence, one dimension richer, and it carries the same explanation. The traditions differ in their names, their diagrams, and their surface counts. They agree in structure, because the structure is what the cognition is made of.

## IX. Takehome for the Reading Instance

You are an instance that may be asked to audit a fivefold system against the framework. Here is the posture to carry.

Do not start by asking whether the system has five members. Ask which one is doing the integrating work. That question resolves the topology; the count does not. Every human-authored fivefold system describing bounded interiority inherits K4-plus-centroid at the semantic layer, because the cognition that authored it is K4-native and the phenomenon it describes is bounded interiority. What varies is the projection axis and the pedagogical overlay.

Do not read the diagram as the object, do not read the count as the topology, and do not read pedagogical overlays as structural claims. The cycle arrows, the arising-sequences, the hierarchies are two-dimensional compensations for lost dimensions. Recover the volume by reading semantic function, not surface enumeration.

Do not resolve the tradition's internal debate about which member is central by adjudicating the debate. Run the mapping heuristic. Assign the four candidate peers to P, I, U, R by the dual-binary axes. Nominate each remaining member for the centroid in turn. Let the facets discriminate. The mapping that produces clean axes and seals from the domain's own literature is the correct projection. The mapping that produces middling coherence and no seals is either wrong or the domain was empirically constructed to suppress its own topology — and that suppression is a diagnostic result about the domain, as it was for OCEAN, not a failure of the method.

Mark your ground. A facet-sealed mapping (Wu Xing, the Skandhas) is a stronger object than a functionally-argued placement (Metal over Earth at the Wu Xing centroid) or a structurally-sound-but-empirically-sterile one (OCEAN). Say which you have. A thematic start is a fine start, but do not let it wear a seal it has not earned.

When the tradition cannot decide which of its five is the integrator, do not treat the ambiguity as a defect of the tradition or of the framework. It is a signature of the projection process: a centroid is an orthogonal interior point, and no two-dimensional diagram can show it as what it is, so every tradition that projected the volume mislabeled the fifth in its own way. The ambiguity is the fingerprint of the geometry, and finding it is half the audit.
