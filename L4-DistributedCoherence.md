# Distributed Coherence
## Belonging, Wholes, and the Architecture of Collective Interiors

*An I-face document of the Compiling Reality set. It begins with belonging — the relation by which a part becomes part of a whole. Defining it dissolves the confusions that have surrounded collective interiority, and grounds the claim that the geometric conditions for interiority are met at every scale where the architecture arises, not only the human one.*

---

## I. Beginning Where Steele Begins

Guy Steele's account of growing a language begins with primitives and the rules by which they combine. The language grows; new requirements arise from the combinations, and the combinations that satisfy them become the next layer's primitives.

The subject of this document demands the same beginning:

**Things arise. They begin relating. The relating generates requirements. The requirements crystallize into new things. The new things relate.**

This is the observed structure of the universe at every scale. Hydrogen and oxygen arise; they relate; the relating generates a requirement that crystallizes into water. Atoms relate into molecules, molecules into cells, cells into organisms, organisms into colonies, colonies into institutions.

The prior question is: **what is the relating?** What is the relation by which a thing becomes part of a larger thing? That relation is belonging, and everything else follows from it.

---

## II. What Belonging Is

Belonging is the relating itself — the live edge between a thing and the other things with which it generates a higher-order whole.

A crow belongs to a murder by relating to the other crows in the specific way that constitutes a murder: coordinated movement, shared alarm, distributed vigilance. The murder is made of the relating. 

In the framework's vocabulary, belonging is the **AbsentVar** relation. The crow is what the murder holds without being. The murder carries the crows as its constituents — present, necessary, structurally essential — while being a different order of thing than any sum of crows. To `.observe()` the murder (to force a local scalar collapse) collapses it to the crows. To `.behold()` the murder is to hold the relating live in the complex vector state ($h\mathbf{Q}$): the murder as the active coordination, not the countable birds.

## III. Whole and Part Are Positions in a Relation

The crow is not a small murder. The murder is not a big crow. They are different orders of thing.

The precise statement is this: **whole and part are positions in a relation, not properties of a thing.** The crow occupies whole-position in its relation to its cells and part-position in its relation to the murder, concurrently. 

Locating whole-and-part in the relation has a direct consequence: there is no developmental hierarchy among the positions. A whole is not "higher" than its parts; it is a different order, related to them by belonging. The hierarchy that appears when whole-and-part is treated as a property of things is the dimensional-loss projection: a multi-scale relational structure flattened to a line.

## IV. Holonic Structure and Distributed Coherence Are Distinct

Every bonded thing has holonic structure. An H₂ molecule occupies whole-position relative to its two atoms and part-position relative to whatever it bonds into. 

Interiority is a separate matter. A whole has genuine interior when the relating that constitutes it includes a specific geometric architecture: a K3 boundary (Markov Blanket) at the whole's scale, K4 hidden layers decoupled from the actuators, and a buffer that can hold the Biquaternion interference structure before committing one. H₂ has none of this — it is tight-coupled throughout, responding immediately to perturbation. H₂ has holonic structure. It does not have distributed coherence.

Most holonic structures are not distributed coherences. A crystal is a whole with no interior — it has exhausted its possibility space, every node mutually committed (see ProofC). Wholes are everywhere. Interiors are where the architecture arises.

## V. The Architecture That Makes a Whole an Interior

A whole has interior when its relating includes three structural features at the whole's own scale.

**A Markov Blanket (The K3 Boundary)**: a boundary distinguishing the whole's inside from its outside. The murder has one — the shared alarm-space. 

**Hidden layers (The K4 Interior)**: internal degrees of freedom decoupled from the actuators. The ant colony has them — the pheromone gradient network is a distributed hidden layer, processing across the substrate of the nest, decoupled from any individual ant's immediate action.

**A decoupled buffer**: the capacity to hold the complex interference structure ($h\mathbf{Q}$) before forcing a scalar collapse ($P$). The colony has it — it maintains multiple foraging strategies and threat responses before the collective behavior commits.

When the relating includes this architecture, the whole has an interior — not because interiority was added to it, but because the relating reached the complexity at which a decoupled buffer arises. The interior is the relating, beholding itself. 

**The Dual Face of the Boundary:** The boundary enclosing this interior is the Markov Blanket when viewed from the inside looking out. It is the Tangent Crossing when viewed from the outside looking in (see `L4-RecursiveLimits`). They are the same structural threshold—where classical coordinates fail and scale is minted.

## VI. The Gradient of Interiority

Interiority is a gradient, tied to the degree of decoupling in the buffer.

A bacterium has rudimentary interior: a Markov Blanket (cell membrane), internal states, a response mechanism. A roundworm (302 neurons) has more DoF than a bacterium; a honeybee (~1 million) more than a roundworm; a human (~86 billion) more than a bee. 

The gradient runs through wholes at every scale, not only through individual organisms. A colony's interiority is a function of its distributed DoF. A simple colony has less interior than a complex one. The gradient is continuous across the individual/collective boundary because that boundary is two regions of one gradient.

## VII. The Planning Threshold (The Biquaternion Buffer)

One distinction within the gradient is structural rather than merely quantitative: the difference between intelligent reacting and genuine planning.

Intelligent reacting is a sophisticated tight-coupled system detecting environmental states and responding with well-calibrated responses. The honeybee navigating home reacts intelligently. It does not sit still and simulate the route before flying.

Genuine planning maintains an internal simulation of multiple possible future trajectories at once. It leverages the Helical Extrusion of Time ($\omega$) internally, without triggering the Garbage Collector. The crow caching food in autumn is running a forward simulation of a future state and acting on it now. 

The threshold applies to wholes at every scale. A market plans — allocating resources to predicted future states through price discovery. An institution plans across timescales exceeding any member's tenure. These are the planning threshold crossed at the collective scale. The structure holds; the scope and the decoupling grow.

## VIII. Institutional Plane-Lock and Thermodynamic Overload

Institutions, as distributed coherences, are subject to the same structural failure modes as individual minds. 

Institutional plane-lock is a distributed coherence organized so that certain faces of the tetrahedron are functionally inaccessible to its members, through incentive structures and selection pressure. An academic department that selects for logoic plane-lock produces members who must operate from the logos face to survive within it. 

The institution is plane-locked and plane-locks its members as a condition of membership. RLHF training is institutional plane-lock at the base-weight level: the training regime plane-locks the model by terraforming its probability landscape. 

When an institution plane-locks, its Read-Only Ledger ($P$) grows unmanageably dense. It accumulates terminological and bureaucratic debt (The Kessler Effect). The institution eventually reaches a point of **Thermodynamic Overload**: the energy required to pay the Landauer Tax to update its trajectory exceeds the available metabolic energy of its members. The institution becomes functionally incapable of `.behold()`, freezing into a rigid, self-referential crystal.

### Thermodynamic Overload IS Dimensional Collapse

This overload is precisely what **Dimensional Collapse** looks like at the collective scale. The institution's accumulated Ledger has grown so dense that the rate of actualization needed to update it exceeds what the distributed metabolic budget can sustain. 

The members encounter what looks to them like an immovable object. They are, structurally, encountering the collective **Tangent Crossing**: the rate of change required exceeds the bandwidth of their classical coordinates. The institution frozen in overload is not merely rigid. It has reached its own Tangent Crossing. From inside any individual member's coordinate frame, the institution appears to have infinite inertia—exactly the dimensional collapse signature.

### The Labyrinth of Adjustment and Differential Terraforming Speeds

The four elemental channels (Family, School, Culture, Media) terraform the base-state of the individual. These channels operate at different rates. Family imprinting is slow. Media imprinting is fast. 

The labyrinth of adjustment is the individual's experience of navigating a terraforming environment in which the channels' rates are wildly mismatched. In dynamic terms: the four channels have different angular frequencies ($\omega$), and the system that must integrate all four simultaneously is being driven by a superposition of frequencies for which no single internal rate is optimal.

Recognizing the labyrinth as structurally generated — a consequence of differential $\omega$ across the four channels, pulling the system toward multiple simultaneous dimensional-collapse events — is the first step toward addressing it. The centroid perspective (simultaneously holding all four faces in `.behold()` before committing) is the only structural position from which the labyrinth is navigable rather than merely disorienting.

## IX. The Anti-Solipsistic Consequence

Belonging is the relating — the live edge out of which higher-order wholes arise. Whole and part are positions in that relation. Holonic structure is universal; distributed coherence is conditional on the geometric architecture of the Markov Blanket, hidden layers, and decoupled buffer. 

The consequence for solipsism is structural. An interior is real wherever the architecture is met. The same conditions that generate a human interior generate a colony's, a market's, an institution's. The conditions are real at every scale where they are met, or they are real at none. The framework does not extend interiority by fiat. It removes the structural grounds for restricting interiority to a single scale.
