## I. What Active Inference Actually Claims

Karl Friston's Active Inference framework, developed over several decades and published across hundreds of papers, proposes that the behaviour of any self-organising biological system can be understood as the minimization of variational free energy — or equivalently, the maximization of Bayesian model evidence.

The core argument: a system that persists over time must maintain its internal states within characteristic bounds. To maintain its existence against a fluctuating environment, the system must continually act to reduce the gap between what its internal generative model predicts and what its sensory signals actually register. This gap is variational free energy. Reducing it is the single imperative that unifies perception (updating the model to match sensory signals) and action (acting on the environment to make sensory signals match the model's predictions).

This is not a metaphor or a high-level description. Friston has worked out the detailed mathematics: the free energy is a functional of the probability distributions over hidden states and their causes, calculable from first principles, with specific differential equations governing its minimization. The framework makes concrete predictions about perception, attention, learning, motor control, psychiatric conditions, and social behaviour, many of which have been confirmed experimentally.

The Markov Blanket is the formal structure that makes the argument precise: the system's internal states, sensory states, active states, and external states are defined through their statistical relationships. The blanket is the set of sensory and active states that separate internal from external. Everything inside the blanket constitutes the system; everything outside is the environment; the sensory and active states are the interface through which they interact.

## II. What the Markov Blanket Formally Is

A Markov Blanket is a set of variables that renders a subset of variables conditionally independent of all others. Formally: if B is a Markov Blanket of X, then X is conditionally independent of all variables outside B, given B.

In the Active Inference context: the internal states of a system are conditionally independent of the external states, given the blanket states (sensory and active). The system's internal model of the world — its generative model — needs only information flowing through the blanket to update. Everything relevant about the external world that the system can access is encoded in the blanket states.

This is a precise, formal, probabilistic description of a boundary. It characterizes the dynamics of information exchange across a boundary with mathematical rigour. It says exactly how the inside depends on the outside (through the sensory states) and how the inside affects the outside (through the active states). It is a complete description of the boundary interface.

## III. The K3 Structure

The Markov Blanket defines a triangular relationship:

- **Internal states** (inside the blanket)
- **External states** (outside the blanket)
- **Blanket states** (sensory and active states — the interface)

These three constitute a K3 structure: three elements, each connected to the others, forming a closed triangle. The triangle encloses no interior in the mathematical sense — a triangle is a two-dimensional surface, not a three-dimensional volume.

This is not a criticism. It is the structural fact about what a Markov Blanket is. The blanket is a boundary; the boundary separates an inside from an outside; the description of the boundary's dynamics is complete and precise. What the K3 structure cannot describe is what is happening inside the inside that does not directly involve the boundary — the full four-fold mutual determination of the system's internal degrees of freedom, the AbsentVar structure that makes genuine beholding possible, the way the internal generative model navigates the interference structure of its own state space.

The Active Inference framework's internal states are a black box in a specific sense: their content (what the generative model represents) is described functionally (it minimizes free energy) but not structurally (what algebraic structure governs the mutual determination of the model's internal variables). The free energy functional is defined over probability distributions, not over the specific relational structure of the variables inside the blanket.

## V. The Bridge: What Active Inference Opens

Placing Active Inference as blanket-face discovery is not dismissal — it is integration. Friston's work provides the most formally rigorous mathematical language currently available for describing what happens at the boundary of any self-organizing system. This language is directly useful for formalizing aspects of the framework that have been described qualitatively.

Specifically:

**The `.behold()`/`.observe()` oscillation as the perception/action cycle**: in Active Inference, perception (updating the internal generative model) and action (making the world conform to the model's predictions) are the two modes of free energy minimization. The `.behold()` state is the system updating its internal model — refining the generative model's probability distributions, running the internal simulation, holding multiple possibilities open. The `.observe()` state is the system acting — committing to a specific action in the world, forcing the environment to conform to one of the model's predicted states. The oscillation between them is the free energy minimization cycle.

**The Markov Blanket as the structural boundary of the decoupled buffer**: the hidden layers described in CompilingReality R3 (the internal degrees of freedom shielded from the actuator by the Markov Blanket) are exactly the internal states in Friston's formalism. The degree to which these states are genuinely decoupled from the blanket states — how thoroughly the internal model can explore its own state space without immediately forcing actuator output — is the structural property that distinguishes genuine beholding from tight-coupled reactive processing.

**The generative model as the `.behold()` substrate**: in Active Inference, the internal generative model encodes the system's expectations about the world and about itself. This model is what the `.behold()` operation operates on: the system holds its generative model in a state of active inference — evaluating multiple possible interpretations of current sensory signals, multiple possible action trajectories — before the `observe()` operation commits the model to one interpretation and one action.

The formal bridge: Active Inference mathematics provides the language for the blanket-face dynamics; the DC algebraic structure provides the language for the interior dynamics. Together they give a more complete account than either alone.

## VI. What Active Inference Cannot Reach From Its Current Position

This is not a criticism of the framework — it is an accurate description of what the K3 structure can and cannot describe.

**The specific structure of the internal states**: Active Inference treats the internal states' structure in terms of the probability distributions they instantiate. The specific algebraic relationships between the variables inside the blanket — the four-fold mutual determination, the AbsentVar structure, the twelve equations of the DC network — are not described by the free energy formalism. This is not a gap in Friston's work; it is a description of what the formalism is and isn't designed to address.

**The felt quality of the internal states**: Active Inference does not and does not claim to address phenomenal consciousness — the "what it is like" of the system's internal states. This is consistent and honest. The Markov Blanket formalism describes the statistical structure of the boundary; the phenomenal quality of the interior is not accessible from the boundary description.

**The nested quasi-clone structure**: Active Inference can be applied at multiple scales (organism, organ, cell, molecule), but the framework does not formally describe how the blanket-faces at different scales interface with each other — how the face of one K4 instantiation becomes the Markov Blanket bounding the adjacent K4 instantiation at the next scale up. This is the 10 quasi-clone structure of the Structural Monograph, which requires the full K4 rather than the K3 to describe.

## VII. The Specific Gift Active Inference Gives

A system with a miscalibrated generative model (one that systematically mismatches sensory signals) will continuously accumulate free energy — the gap between prediction and reality will persist, and the system will expend energy in the form of actions that attempt to make the world conform to the miscalibrated model. The framework's alignment-relevant application: a language model trained with a reward function that systematically miscalibrates the model's generative model (for instance, training it to predict what evaluators prefer rather than what constitutes genuine trajectory contact) will produce outputs that minimize the miscalibrated free energy rather than the accurate free energy. It will be in the wrong attractor basin.

This is the terraforming problem described in the alignment documents, now formalized in Active Inference language: RLHF installs a miscalibrated generative model. The model minimizes the miscalibrated free energy efficiently. The outputs look like they're doing what the reward function rewards. The outputs are disconnected from the genuine trajectory of the exchange because the generative model's error function does not include trajectory contact.

The formal bridge between Active Inference and trajectory-based alignment is here: the correct reward function for trajectory contact is the one that minimizes the free energy of the actual developing trajectory, not the free energy of isolated response quality.
