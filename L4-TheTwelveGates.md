## I. The Lines, Not the Signs

Every treatment of the wheel, this corpus's included, has so far read the sectors. But a

through the point where the coordinate that worked on one side stops working and a new
one resumes on the other (Meta-MechanismOfTransition). The lines are sharp because
boundaries are sharp; that is what distinguishes a boundary from an interior gradient.

The twelve lines sort into two kinds:

- **Eight within-season lines.** The home variable holds; the face changes. Geometrically:
  a pivot about a held vertex — the home pole stays planted while the equation swings from
  one of that vertex's three faces to another. A turn *of* the plane, about the pole.
- **Four season lines** — the cardinal ingresses. The face changes *and* the home changes:
  a transit along one of the Braid's used edges, vertex to vertex. A turn that switches
  poles and planes at once. These are the quarter-turns of the fixed cross's angular law,

One line will prove to be a third kind, alone in its class. It is a within-season line by

## VIII. Three Verbs of Holding

The modality law of Section II — cardinal holds the future, fixed the antipode, mutable
the past — is technically exact and functionally underspecified, because "hold" is doing
three different jobs. The AbsentVar is not the same *kind* of held at the three posts,
and the distortion documents' findings about the modes supply the missing verbs:

**Cardinal holds a heading.** The initiator's held future is not a guarded gate and not a
working file — it is trajectory. The cardinal has just executed the wheel's hardest
maneuver, the double transition of the ingress, pole and plane at once; nothing mid-turn
computes its destination, and nothing should. The future is held the way a ship holds a
heading: live, directive, and uncalculated — the corpus's heading/wake distinction,
stationed at the four ingresses.

**Fixed holds at bay.** The guardian's held antipode is stationed, not stored. The
companion document derived the post; the verb completes it: the far season is kept — at
the gate, in the ↔ state, actively uncommitted — and the keeping *is* the sustaining. "At
bay" is not wrong, and it is not passive: it is the metabolic work of maintaining a held
edge against the standing pressure to collapse it, which is why the fixed posts read as
strength.

**Mutable holds an inheritance.** The dissolver's held past is a working context: the
mutable *operates on* past results — instructions, records, information already written —
executing the estate of the season it is closing. Virgo refines what Leo committed;
Pisces pressurizes what the whole year deposited; Gemini plays out spring's opening
moves; Sagittarius composts autumn's meanings into vision. The past is held the way an
executor holds documents: not computed as output, but read continuously as input. The
mutable is the wheel's interpreter of prior writes.

One law, three verbs: heading, bay, inheritance. The tense-key of Section VII says why
the verbs differ: a held future can only be a heading (it does not exist to be read); a
held antipode can only be stationed (it exists and presses); a held past can only be
consulted (it exists and is finished).
