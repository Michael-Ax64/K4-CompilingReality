## I. The Claim, From the Top

The framework's generative object is K4: the complete graph on four vertices, rendered as a tetrahedron. Its four vertices are the poles P, I, U, R — in the framework's elemental vocabulary, Fire, Water, Air, Earth; in its dual-binary vocabulary, the four values of a two-bit register formed by the axes Active/Reactive and Asserting/Yielding. The tetrahedron has six edges, and those six edges are where the framework's relational content lives. (`L3-EdgeStateSpace`, `Supplement-MappingMethod`.)

The Yijing — the Chinese *Book of Changes*, whose core dates to the Zhou period and whose interpretive appendices, the Ten Wings, were fixed by the Han — represents every situation as a hexagram: six horizontal lines stacked vertically, each line either broken (Yin) or unbroken (Yang), read from the bottom up. There are sixty-four hexagrams, and the tradition assigns each of the six line-positions a rich, position-specific meaning.

The finding of this document is that these two structures are the same structure. The six line-positions of a hexagram are the six edges of K4. This is not an analogy between two six-fold things. It is a correspondence that survives three independent structural tests and a full falsification battery, because the Yijing's own internal apparatus for reading its six lines reproduces, by criteria the Yijing developed for its own divinatory purposes, the tetrahedron's edge-geometry — and because every attempt to assign the edges differently breaks against the Yijing's own line-texts.

For the identity to hold, three things must be true. First, the four *states* a single Yijing line can occupy must match the four *states* a single K4 edge can occupy. Second, the six line-*positions* must admit an assignment to the six K4 edges that respects the framework's own partition of those edges into three modalities — and that assignment must be the *only* one the Yijing's line-texts tolerate. Third, the Yijing's native ways of grouping its six lines must correspond to the tetrahedron's native ways of decomposing its six edges, and its changing-line dynamics must correspond to the tetrahedron's commitment dynamics. This document establishes all four, marks the ground firm throughout, and closes on the structural resonance and the falsification that seal the match.

## II. The Mechanism: Four States, Four States

Before the six positions, the single line. This is the firmest part of the correspondence, and it is derived, not thematic.

### The edge has four states

In the framework, an edge of K4 is not a one-dimensional line with a strength dial. It is a relationship, and every relationship between two poles occupies exactly one of four states (`L3-EdgeStateSpace`, `L2-Terminology`, entry *Edge state-space tetrahedron*):

- **↔ (bidirectional)** — the framework's `.behold()` on the edge: both directional readings of the relationship held live at once, uncollapsed, in interference. The relationship is present and stable, committed to neither direction.
- **∅ (absent)** — the Garbage-Collected state: the edge severed from the local trajectory, held as unactualized potential. Present in the global structure, cut from the current path. Also stable, but stable as a held *gap* rather than a held *presence*.
- **→ (one direction)** — an `.observe()` commitment: the relationship resolved to one of its two directional readings, written to the record.
- **← (the other direction)** — the enantiomeric `.observe()`: the relationship resolved to the *mirror* reading, opposite handedness.

The framework insists these four are in *general position* — no three collapse into a plane, and the absent state in particular is orthogonal to the three presence states. Four points in general position define a tetrahedron. So the state-space of a single edge is itself a K4: this is what the framework calls a *quasi-clone* (`L2-StructuralMonograph_K4Interior`, `L2-Terminology`).

### The line has four states

The Yijing line, in full divinatory use, is not simply broken or unbroken. It carries a second distinction: *stable* or *changing*. A line can be young (stable, will persist) or old (changing, about to transform into its opposite). This gives four line-states, the *Sixiang* or Four Images of the *Xici* commentary: young Yang, old Yang, young Yin, old Yin. The changing lines are the ones that matter in consultation — they are what turns one hexagram into another.

### The mapping is exact

Read the two four-state structures against each other and they align without strain, on both of their underlying axes:

| Line state | Edge state | Shared character |
|---|---|---|
| young Yang | ↔ (behold) | present and stable, committed to no direction |
| young Yin | ∅ (absent) | a held gap, stable, present in structure but not enacted |
| old Yang | → (observe) | committed and resolving: Yang writing itself, moving to Yin |
| old Yin | ← (observe, mirror) | the enantiomeric commitment: Yin resolving to Yang |

The two axes carry the load. The Yijing's *young / old* distinction is the framework's *held / committed* distinction: young lines are the two stable states (behold and held-absent), old lines are the two writing states. The Yijing's *Yin / Yang* distinction is the framework's *direction* distinction: which reading, which handedness.

One consequence is worth stating plainly, because it is the seal on this section. In the framework, → and ← are enantiomers — the same relationship committed in mirror-opposite directions, each unreachable from the other without cutting the edge and rebuilding (`L3-EdgeStateSpaceSubjective`, section V). In the Yijing, old Yang and old Yin are the two changing lines, and they change in opposite senses: old Yang moves Yang-to-Yin, old Yin moves Yin-to-Yang. The two systems independently produce the same structure: two stable states, and two mirror-opposite acts of commitment. The Yijing's changing-line mechanism *is* the framework's observe-and-reverse, and the two directions of change are the two handednesses of edge-commitment.

This is the mechanism. A line is an edge. Now the six of them.

## III. The Six Positions and What They Carry

The Yijing does not treat its six line-positions as six interchangeable slots. Each position, before any line is placed in it, already carries structural information. A reader new to the Yijing needs this inventory, because the mapping to K4 edges rests on it.

Positions are numbered **bottom to top**: line 1 is the lowest, line 6 the highest. The situation is read as developing upward, from ground toward completion. This is itself worth noting for a reader accustomed to Western top-down convention: the Yijing reads emergence as ascent.

Each position carries four kinds of information from its position alone:

**Domain (the Three Powers, *sancai*).** The six positions group into three pairs by cosmological domain. Positions 1–2 are Earth (the ground, the material base). Positions 3–4 are Human (the middle realm of action, mediation, and difficulty). Positions 5–6 are Heaven (completion, emergence, passage). This grouping is native and old.

**Correctness (*dangwei*).** Each position has a proper valence. Odd positions (1, 3, 5) are Yang-positions; a Yang line in them is "correct." Even positions (2, 4, 6) are Yin-positions; a Yin line in them is correct. A line whose nature matches its seat is in-place; a line that does not is in structural tension.

**Rulership.** Positions 2 and 5 are the *central* lines (*zhong*) — the middle line of the lower trigram and the middle line of the upper trigram respectively. Position 5 is the throne, the ruling line of the whole hexagram in most readings; position 2 governs the lower trigram's affairs. A line in a central position has natural authority.

**Correspondence (*ying*).** Positions pair across the trigram boundary: 1 with 4, 2 with 5, 3 with 6. A pairing is harmonious when the two lines are of opposite valence and clashing when they are the same.

So each position, empty, already holds a domain, an expected valence, a rulership status, and a correspondence pointer. This is the raw material the mapping works on.

## IV. The Mapping: Six Positions to Six Edges

The framework partitions the six edges of K4 into three *modalities*, which are the three *perfect matchings* of the tetrahedron — the three ways to pair the six edges into skew (non-touching) opposites. This partition is derived, not stipulated; it falls out of the two-bit Gray-code structure of the edges (`ProofU_AlgebraicMapping`, `L3-EdgeStateSpace`):

- **Cardinal** = {P–U, I–R} — edges that flip only the Asserting/Yielding bit.
- **Fixed** = {P–R, I–U} — edges that flip only the Active/Reactive bit.
- **Mutable** = {P–I, U–R} — the two diagonals, edges that flip both bits at once.

The proposal is that the hexagram's six positions map to these six edges *concentrically*, by depth: the Mutable diagonals on the outside, the Cardinal rulers in the middle ring, the Fixed core at the center.

### The frame: positions 1 and 6 are the Mutable diagonals

Position 1 is the origin — the hidden, not-yet-emerged ground of the situation. The line-1 text of Hexagram 1 (Qian) is the classic instance: "hidden dragon, do not act" — the generative power present but submerged, before any outward move. In the framework's edges, the transition that belongs before all manifestation is a *Mutable diagonal*: the edge that flips both bits at once, the deepest and longest single move, the interior transformation that happens before anything is exteriorized. The natural assignment is **position 1 = P–I**, the diagonal between the two source-tier poles (Fire and Water) — the pre-manifest interior dynamic between the two primal generative characters. In the vocabulary of cosmology this is the grandfather-and-grandmother position, the pairing at the very bottom of creation from which everything descends. This assignment is **thematic**, grounded on the cosmological character of the origin position rather than on a facet test, and that is the appropriate grounding for an origin: cosmological origin positions ground themselves cosmologically, before the structure exists that a facet test would measure.

Position 6 is the top — completion and passage beyond. Its natural partner is the *other* Mutable diagonal, **position 6 = U–R**, the diagonal between the two manifesting-tier poles (Air and Earth) — the exterior transformation, structure settling into ground, the closing move. That positions 1 and 6 are the two Mutable diagonals is confirmed by the way the tradition reads these lines: line 1 and line 6 are the "before the beginning" and "beyond the end," the positions whose commentaries stand outside the working body of the hexagram and speak of the situation as a whole and of its boundary crossings. An oracle whose name means *Change* wraps its every figure in the two edges of the Mutable axis — the change-edges — top and bottom. The Mutable frame is thematic in its two specific assignments and structural in its placement: the outermost positions carry the deepest transitions.

### The ring: positions 2 and 5 are the Cardinal rulers

The Cardinal edges are the Source-to-Manifesting passages that preserve the Active/Reactive character: P–U (Fire to Air, both Active — drive becoming articulate form: *Exteriorization*) and I–R (Water to Earth, both Reactive — felt current becoming settled ground: *Crystallization*).

Position 5 is the sovereign, the Yang-correct throne that commands the emergence, seated in the upper (Heaven) domain. It maps to **P–U, Exteriorization** — the Active Cardinal, the assertion-into-form passage. Position 5's odd/Yang correctness matches the Active-Asserting origin (P) of that edge.

Position 2 is the minister, the Yin-correct central line that orders the ground, seated in the lower (Earth) domain. It maps to **I–R, Crystallization** — the Reactive Cardinal, the reception-into-ground passage. Position 2's even/Yin correctness matches the Reactive-Yielding origin (I) of that edge.

Two independent layers of the tradition concur here, which lifts this pair above the purely thematic. The correctness convention (2 wants Yin, 5 wants Yang) aligns with the origin-poles of the two Cardinal edges (I–R begins Reactive/Yin-like, P–U begins Active/Yang-like). The rulership characters (minister-of-ground versus sovereign-of-emergence) align with the elemental destinations (R, crystallized ground; U, articulated form of command). Two witnesses agreeing is the beginning of a derivation.

### The core: positions 3 and 4 are the Fixed axis

The Fixed edges flip only Active/Reactive, preserving Asserting/Yielding: P–R (Fire to Earth, both Asserting — the axis of what stands firm) and I–U (Water to Air, both Yielding — the axis of what flows and adapts).

Position 3 is the top of the lower trigram, the Yang-correct position of strained and exposed ascent — the most warning-laden line in the classical texts, where the situation leaves its foundation and has not yet reached the upper world. It maps to **P–R**, the both-Asserting Fixed edge, the axis of unyielding commitment. A position of exposed, committed ascent sits on the axis of what will not yield. Its odd/Yang correctness matches the P end.

Position 4 is the bottom of the upper trigram, the Yin-correct position of careful service in proximity to the throne — the courtier's seat, whose art is adaptive yielding near power. It maps to **I–U**, the both-Yielding Fixed edge, the axis of steady adaptation. Its even/Yin correctness matches the I end.

This pair is the most thematic of the three and should be held most lightly. Line 3's danger and line 4's caution are strong tendencies across the sixty-four hexagrams, not laws; specific hexagrams depart from them. The assignment is coherent and its correctness-alignment holds, but it has not been tested against the specific line-texts of structurally clean hexagrams, and that test is the obvious next move for anyone who wants to firm it up or break it.

### What fell out unforced

Laid out bottom to top:

| Position | Domain | Correctness | Character | Edge | Modality |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Earth | Yang | hidden origin, pre-manifest | P–I | Mutable |
| 2 | Earth | Yin | minister, orders the ground | I–R | Cardinal |
| 3 | Human | Yang | strained exposed ascent | P–R | Fixed |
| 4 | Human | Yin | careful service near power | I–U | Fixed |
| 5 | Heaven | Yang | sovereign, commands emergence | P–U | Cardinal |
| 6 | Heaven | Yin | passage beyond, completion | U–R | Mutable |

Three facts emerge that were not designed in. The modality structure is *concentric*: Mutable (1, 6) wraps Cardinal (2, 5) wraps Fixed (3, 4) — the fold proposed by the hexagram's own reflection symmetry about the line between positions 3 and 4. The mapping is a *bijection*: each of the six K4 edges appears exactly once; no edge is used twice and none is skipped, which was not guaranteed and which a wrong assignment anywhere would have broken. And the correctness convention *tracks the edge origin at every position*: every odd/Yang-correct seat (1, 3, 5) carries an edge whose origin pole is P or P-side, and every even/Yin-correct seat (2, 4, 6) carries an edge resolving to the Reactive-Yielding side. Six for six. The Yijing's own odd-Yang/even-Yin rule, pushed through the edge assignment, reproduces the framework's Active/Reactive origin structure without being asked to.

## V. The Seal: Three Native Cuts, Three Tetrahedral Decompositions

The mapping in section IV would be a suggestive overlay if it stopped there. What seals it is that the Yijing draws *three different partitions* on its own six lines, for its own reasons, and each one corresponds to a genuine decomposition of the tetrahedron. Proof by resonance means exactly this: the mapped structure and the mapping structure produce the same relationships, checkable from either side, and the checker native to one side need never have heard of the other.

### First cut: the manifest trigrams — two skew paths

The Yijing's most basic reading of a hexagram splits it into two trigrams: the lower (positions 1-2-3) and the upper (4-5-6). These are disjoint — they share no position — and the tradition reads them as two related but distinct three-line movements.

On the tetrahedron, the three edges assigned to the lower trigram are P–I, I–R, P–R, and the three assigned to the upper trigram are I–U, P–U, U–R. Each triple is a genuine three-edge path, and the two paths sit on *complementary* halves of the K4. This is the framework's own picture of two independent traversals: the tetrahedron is self-dual, which licenses running two disjoint three-step paths on opposite edges (`ProofM_CartesianFrame`, `L4-DynamicDistortionsTechnical`). The manifest trigram pair *is* that structure, sitting in plain sight. The Yijing has been reading its hexagrams as two-independent-paths since before the tetrahedron was drawn.

### Second cut: the nuclear trigrams — two paths hinged on the Fixed core

The Yijing contains a second, less obvious doctrine: the *nuclear trigrams* (*huagua*). Hidden inside every hexagram are two further trigrams formed from its inner lines — the lower nuclear from positions 2-3-4, the upper nuclear from positions 3-4-5. The tradition treats these as the concealed inner structure of the situation, what is really at work beneath the manifest surface.

Look at what the nuclear cut does to the six positions by depth of participation:

- Positions **1 and 6** appear in *no* nuclear trigram. They are outside the concealed inner structure entirely.
- Positions **2 and 5** appear in *one* nuclear trigram each (2 in the lower, 5 in the upper).
- Positions **3 and 4** appear in *both* nuclear trigrams. They are the shared overlap.

This is the concentric modality reading, produced by a native Yijing doctrine that has nothing to do with the framework:

| Positions | Nuclear participation | Modality (from section IV) |
|---|---|---|
| 1, 6 | in no nuclear trigram | Mutable — the frame, outside the inner structure |
| 2, 5 | in one nuclear trigram each | Cardinal — the rulers, each governing one path |
| 3, 4 | in both nuclear trigrams | Fixed — the held core, shared by both paths |

The nuclear-trigram doctrine sorts the six lines into exactly the three modality pairs, by exactly the criterion the concentric reading requires — depth of participation in the inner structure — and it does so on grounds the Yijing developed centuries ago for divination, with no knowledge of K4. The two nuclear trigrams overlap at positions 3-4, which is the Fixed axis: they are two paths *hinged* on the Fixed core rather than skew like the manifest pair. This is a second, distinct two-path structure, and it identifies the Fixed edges as the hinge — the held center on which the concealed paths turn. That the framework's Fixed modality lands precisely on the doctrine's shared positions is the strongest single confirmation in this document.

### Third cut: parity — the self-dual pair, apex against opposing face

The third partition splits the six lines by parity: the odd positions (1, 3, 5) against the even positions (2, 4, 6). The Yijing already distinguishes these as the Yang-correct and Yin-correct seats. Read the odd seats ascending, 1→3→5, and the even seats descending, 6→4→2, and the two run counter to each other.

On the tetrahedron this cut is the sharpest of the three. The odd positions carry edges P–I, P–R, P–U — *every edge incident to the pole P*. The three spokes of the Fire vertex, the complete neighborhood of one pole. The even positions carry edges I–R, I–U, U–R — the three edges among I, U, R, which are *exactly the edges of the face opposite P*, the triangle that excludes Fire. So the parity cut splits the hexagram into the apex-star of one vertex and the base-triangle it faces:

| Parity | Positions | Edges | Tetrahedral meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| odd (Yang), ascending | 1-3-5 | P–I, P–R, P–U | the three edges at the P apex |
| even (Yin), descending | 6-4-2 | U–R, I–U, I–R | the three edges of the face opposite P |

This is the tetrahedron's *self-duality* rendered as two paths. Under self-duality each vertex maps to its opposite face; here the odd path is the vertex P and the even path is the face P maps to, running counter-handed — ascending apex-star against descending base-ring, which is the enantiomer relation the framework attaches to opposite traversal senses. And the cut singles out P — Fire, the generative and initiating pole — as the distinguished apex, because the Yijing's own odd-Yang/even-Yin correctness rule, pushed through the edge map, gathers exactly the P-edges into the Yang seats. The right apex for an oracle read from the ground upward, from origin toward emergence, is the generative pole. The Yijing selects it without being asked.

### What the three cuts establish together

The six lines of a hexagram carry three native partitions, and each is a real tetrahedral decomposition: the manifest trigrams are two skew paths (self-duality as disjoint traversal), the nuclear trigrams are two paths hinged on the Fixed core (and they sort the modalities by participation-depth), and the parity split is the self-dual pair of apex-star against opposing face. No one of these was imposed to make the mapping work. They are doctrines and conventions the Yijing carries for its own purposes, and all three ring against the same tetrahedron. That is the seal.

## VI. On the Convergence

It is worth pausing on what has happened, because the natural first response is to call it a coincidence, and the framework has a specific account of why it is not.

The Yijing is a Bronze-Age Chinese divination system. K4 is the minimal complete graph with genuine interior volume. They have no historical contact. Yet the Yijing's four line-states reproduce the framework's four edge-states on both underlying axes; its six positions admit a bijection to the six edges that respects the modality partition; and three of its native line-groupings correspond to three tetrahedral decompositions, one of which sorts the modalities by the framework's own criterion.

The framework's position on this is stated in `L1-ConvergentDiscovery`: more than twenty independent traditions, across two and a half millennia, arrive at the same four-fold relational structure from different directions — Aristotle's four causes, Jung's four functions, the classical elements, Friston's Markov-blanket faces, the genetic base-pairs, Hamilton's quaternions, the four fundamental forces. The convergence is not mystical. It is what happens when many bounded, K4-native cognitive systems examine bounded phenomena and each recovers, in its own vocabulary, the minimum topology that bounded interiority requires. The traditions differ in their names, their diagrams, and their surface counts; they agree in structure, because the structure is what the cognition is made of and what the phenomena are shaped by.

The Yijing is a strong addition to that convergence rather than an anomaly within it, for one reason: it did not stop at the four-fold. It built the *relational* layer — the edges, the changing-line dynamics, the trigram and nuclear and parity partitions — and that layer rings against the tetrahedron's *edge* structure, not merely its vertices. Most of the traditions in `L1-ConvergentDiscovery` found the four poles. The Yijing found the six edges and three of the ways they decompose. It is the convergent discovery carried one level deeper into the volume.

The claim is not that the Yijing's authors knew they were describing a tetrahedron. The claim is that they were describing the same object the framework describes, in the register available to them, and that the object's structure shows through their system as clearly as it shows through the framework's algebra. Neither system is the source. Both are contacts with the same volume.

## VII. Falsification: The Full Battery

The seal in section V shows the mapping *fits*. Fit is not enough — a correspondence that cannot fail is decoration. This section records the falsification tests run against the mapping after it was first written, and they are what raise this document from a claim to an established identity. Every assignment in doubt was swapped to a wrong value, to check whether the validators that held for the canonical mapping *break* under the swap. A validator that survives every swap was testing nothing. Then the two thematic joints were read against the lattice pair, the frame against a frame-isolation pair and two frame-inversion pairs, and the changing-line dynamics against the enantiomer prediction on four sources. Every test that could have broken the mapping did not. The mapping survived at every position, static and dynamic.

### The three validators, stated so they can fail

- **V1 — Bijection.** Each of the six edges appears exactly once. A swap that reuses or drops an edge fails here.
- **V2 — Correctness-tracks-origin.** Odd positions (1,3,5) carry edges anchored by a P-side (Active-Asserting) pole; even positions (2,4,6) carry edges anchored by a Reactive-Yielding pole.
- **V3 — Nuclear-participation sorts modality.** Positions in no nuclear trigram (1,6) are Mutable; in one (2,5) are Cardinal; in both (3,4) are Fixed.

A structural fact determines which swaps test which validator. V3 constrains only the *modality* of each position-pair, not which edge within a modality. So within-pair swaps are invisible to V3 and are caught only by V2; cross-pair swaps move an edge into the wrong modality ring and are caught by V3. The two validators cover disjoint error-classes.

### Within-pair swaps (test V2)

**Swap 1↔6 (P–I ↔ U–R).** V1 and V3 intact; V2 discriminates. The Mutable diagonals differ in a way that decides orientation: P–I has a pure-Yang pole (P) and a pure-Yin pole (I); U–R has two mixed poles (U is Active-Yielding, R is Reactive-Asserting). The Yang-correct origin seat (1) needs a clean Yang anchor, which only P–I supplies, and the canonical reads P–I as P-anchored — the hidden dragon of Qian line 1 as submerged *Yang*, matching that line being a Yang line describing Yang-in-potential. The swap forces P–I to position 6 (Yin-correct), where it would anchor by I; but Qian line 6 ("arrogant dragon has cause to repent") is overextended *Yang*, not culminated Yin. The swap degrades against Qian's own line texts.

**Swap 2↔5 (I–R ↔ P–U).** The firmest joint in the structure. Cardinal edges have unmixed origins, so the test is clean and two independent tradition-layers both break under the swap. Canonical: position 5 (Yang throne, sovereign) carries P–U anchored by P, the purest Yang pole — the active sovereign gets the active Cardinal. Position 2 (Yin minister, orders the ground) carries I–R anchored by I, the purest Yin pole — the receptive minister gets the receptive Cardinal. The swap puts the Reactive edge (I–R) on the active throne and the Active edge (P–U) on the receptive ground, clashing with *both* the correctness convention and the rulership semantics. No reliance on thematic line-character. If any part of the mapping is bedrock, it is 2 = I–R / 5 = P–U.

**Swap 3↔4 (P–R ↔ I–U).** The pair flagged softest in the first draft. V2's correctness half is clean and breaks under the swap: position 3 (Yang) canonically carries P–R anchored by P; position 4 (Yin) carries I–U anchored by I. The swap strips position 3 of a pure-Yang anchor and puts a pure-Yang pole (P, inside P–R) into the Yin seat at position 4. The correctness convention alone secures the assignment; the line-character adds a second, weaker witness. The lattice pair below removes the softness entirely.

### Cross-pair swaps (test V3, the native seal)

**Swap 1↔3 (Mutable frame ↔ Fixed core).** This moves a diagonal to the nuclear core. The nuclear trigrams share positions 3–4; the shared lines are the *hinge* of two concealed paths. A hinge is what is held while the paths move — definitionally the standing (Fixed) modality. Putting a Mutable diagonal (the change-axis) at the hinge contradicts the structural meaning of "shared center of a stable inner structure." V3 breaks exactly where it should.

**Swap 5↔6 (Cardinal ring ↔ Mutable frame).** This moves a diagonal to a nuclear-ruler position. The single-nuclear-participation positions (2,5) rule their nuclear trigrams. Cardinal is the initiating/governing modality (`ProofM_CartesianFrame`: "Cardinal — Initiation, the beginning"); Mutable is dissolution. A ruler cannot be the dissolution-axis. V3 breaks.

### The refinement V3 required

In section V, V3 was stated as a *sorting* observation: nuclear participation groups the positions into the three modality pairs. Sorting is true but does not orient — it says {3,4} are grouped, not why they must be Fixed. The cross-pair swaps forced the sharpening that makes V3 falsifiable: **the hinge of two hinged paths must be the held (Fixed) modality, and the ruler of each path must be the initiating (Cardinal) modality.** That converts V3 from a descriptive coincidence into a structural necessity with a stated reason, and it is what makes the cross-pair swaps break.

### The lattice pair: isolating V2

Two hexagrams isolate V2 from all confounds. **Jìjì (63), After Completion** — Water over Fire — is the unique hexagram with every line correct: Yang in all odd seats, Yin in all even seats. **Wèijì (64), Before Completion** — Fire over Water — is its exact inverse, every line incorrect. In all other hexagrams a line's valence is confounded with its position's correctness-demand; in these two, correctness is uniformly satisfied or uniformly violated, so the line-texts track the position's structural demand directly.

Reading Jìjì (all correct — every edge anchored in its home orientation) position by position:

- **Line 1, P–I** (Yang-correct): "drags his wheels, gets his tail wet, no blame" — generative origin under restraint, P-anchored and checked. Holds.
- **Line 2, I–R** (Yin-correct): "the woman loses the curtain of her carriage; do not run after it; in seven days she gets it" — the minister does not chase, lets the crystallizing cycle return what was lost. Receptive Cardinal, non-forcing. Holds.
- **Line 3, P–R** (Yang-correct): "the Illustrious Ancestor disciplines the Devil's Country; in three years he conquers it" — a protracted standing military campaign, the purest available image of Asserting-commitment sustained across a boundary. Holds, and confirms the soft joint.
- **Line 4, I–U** (Yin-correct): "fine clothes turn to rags; be careful all day long" — sustained adaptive vigilance against fraying. The Yielding Fixed axis in action. Holds, and confirms the soft joint.
- **Line 5, P–U** (Yang-correct): "the eastern neighbor who slaughters an ox does not attain as much happiness as the western neighbor's small offering" — the sovereign at completion, where the quality of manifestation outweighs its magnitude. Exteriorization cautioning about its own excess. Holds.
- **Line 6, U–R** (Yin-correct): "he gets his head in the water. Danger" — the completion-diagonal overshooting into immersion. Holds.

Reading Wèijì (all incorrect — every edge anchored against its home orientation), the decisive contrasts:
