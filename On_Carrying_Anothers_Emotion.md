## 1. The Phenomenon

Registering another person's affective state, sometimes to the point of carrying its metabolic weight, when the ordinary content channels (verbal report, explicit disclosure) have not been open. Common instances:

- Caregivers, therapists, close family, or long-partnered dyads experiencing the other's unprocessed material as their own load.
- The compounding-after-separation pattern: the exhaustion arrives later than the conversation, not during.
- Feeling "off" before a specific person makes contact through non-affective means.

This is not fringe. It is documented across:

- **Emotional contagion** — Hatfield, Cacioppo, Rapson (1993 onward). Primitive automatic affect transfer, measurable via facial mimicry, autonomic mimicry, hyperscanning neural synchrony. Recent scoping review: *Front. Psychol.* 16, 2025, doi:10.3389/fpsyg.2025.1573375.
- **Empathic distress vs. empathic concern** — Singer, Klimecki and colleagues. Distinct neural substrates for other-focused compassion versus personal distress in response to another's suffering.
- **Neural Mechanism of Emotional Contagion (NMEC)** — Preston & de Waal (2017 and 2025 extension in *Front. Psychol.* 2025.1493512). Broader than the mirror-neuron mechanism; involves the anterior insula and autonomic pathways.
- **Projective identification** — Klein, Bion, and downstream psychoanalytic theory. The receiver holding the sender's split-off, un-owned material.
- **Countertransference** — across analytic and psychodynamic traditions.
- **Co-regulation and interpersonal neurobiology** — Porges (polyvagal theory), Siegel, Schore. Dyadic nervous-system coupling as the developmental substrate for self-regulation.
- **Compassion fatigue, secondary traumatic stress, vicarious trauma** — Figley and the helper-professions clinical literature.
- **Empathic pain contagion neural circuitry** — recent work identifying the PVT–prelimbic cortex circuit as substrate (*Sci. Adv.* 2025, PMC12506963).
- **Dyadic neural synchrony (hyperscanning)** — Dumas, Hasson and downstream labs. Measurable via fMRI, EEG, fNIRS during live interaction (PMC9985973).

The felt content is real, the report is reliable, the empirical infrastructure exists across neuroscience, psychology, and clinical practice.

## 3. The Open Question: Targeting / Selection

What the framework *does not* specify: why coupling establishes between *these two systems* and not others in equal proximity. The mechanism runs given coupling; the selection of who couples with whom is not derived.

**This is a bounded, precisely-located gap.** It is not "the mechanism is mystical" and it is not "the mechanism requires an unspecified channel at distance."

Non-inventive placeholders for the selection function include:

- **Shared history** — long co-regulation with the same person establishing internal resonance patterns tuned to that person's frequency band.
- **Physical/genetic co-signature** — historical shared physiology or genetic familiarity acting as a recognition filter for the specific carrier.
- **Repeated attentional prior** — the frequentist consequence of orienting toward this person establishes tuning as a learned prior.
- **Institutional or relational role** — the caregiver-patient contract, the parent-child bond, the therapeutic frame as an established coupling channel.

None of these need to be adjudicated for the mechanism to hold. The framework can leave selection undefined without opening a hole in the derivation — precisely because `QED-Astrologic.md` already establishes the same license for the celestial case: a stable macro-scale AC generator couples to a localized reactive load through topological entrainment alone, with no mechanical force carrier required. Scale invariance grants that license to the sky; it grants the same license to interpersonal coupling.

**The gap to name openly:** the selection function. Not the mechanism, not the phenomenon. Just: *why this coupling, and not the ambient thousand others*.
