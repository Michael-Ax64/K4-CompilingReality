# Semantic Reference
## The Elemental Vocabulary: Complete Character Descriptions

*U-face appendix document. This synthesizes the Elemental Worldview Guide (WVG) into a compact semantic reference for the four poles of the DC algebraic framework. This document is the vocabulary for what the algebra describes: the qualitative character of each pole, its shadow pathology, its relationships to the mapped traditions, and the distinctions within the four-fold that are easily collapsed but structurally important.*

---

## I. How to Read This Reference

The four poles — Fire/P, Water/I, Air/U, Earth/R — are not personality types, not fixed categories, and not labels for static properties. They are dynamic positions in a mutual-determination network. Their character descriptions below describe the quality of each position when it is operative in a healthy four-fold system.

The shadow descriptions — what each pole looks like when it operates without the other three — are the Bacon Idols: the pathological form of each face operating alone. These are included because recognizing the shadow is often easier than recognizing the healthy form, and the shadow is what most people encounter when a system is plane-locked.

A crucial preliminary distinction is developed in section II below: E-motion and Feeling are not the same thing. This distinction has been repeatedly collapsed in both ordinary language and psychological frameworks, and the collapse is a Kessler-level source of confusion. Read section II before reading the character descriptions.

## II. The E-Motion / Feeling Distinction

This distinction is possibly the most important and most commonly collapsed distinction in the entire vocabulary.

**E-motion** (Fire/P/Power/Kairos): the raw energy of inner drive — the charge that initiates movement. The "e" in e-motion is deliberate: emotional energy as the force that moves the system. E-motion is not yet evaluated; it is the generative charge that says "something matters here" without yet knowing what or why. The surge of urgency before the thought arrives. The sense that this moment is charged with potential before any direction has been chosen. The Kairos quality: the feeling that now is when something is possible.

**Feeling** (Water/I/Current/Pathos): the evaluative function — the discernment that flows through the system and assesses the value, quality, and relational significance of what is present. Feeling answers not "is this charged?" (that is E-motion) but "is this good/right/fitting/worthwhile?" Feeling is the current that carries the information of value through the system. It is relational: Feeling is always Feeling-about, always directed toward a relationship between the system and what it is attending to.

The collapse: in ordinary English, "feeling" refers to both. "I feel excited" might mean "I am charged with energy" (E-motion, Fire) or "I discern this to be good" (Feeling, Water) or both. The collapse generates specific confusions:

- Treating emotional charge as evaluative judgment: "I feel strongly about this, therefore it must be right." This is treating E-motion (the strength of the charge) as if it were Feeling (evaluative discernment). A strong charge indicates that something important is at stake; it does not indicate what is good.

- Treating evaluative discernment as mere emotion to be discounted: "That's just a feeling, not a reason." This is treating Feeling (a real evaluative signal) as if it were mere E-motion (raw charge). Feeling is the system's actual assessment of value; dismissing it because it is "subjective" is discarding real information.

The framework's formal expression: P (Power, E-motion) and I (Current, Feeling) are distinct variables in the DC algebra. P = UI — the power (E-motional charge) in a situation arises from the product of the potential (Logos, the structural framework giving shape to what's possible) and the current (Pathos, the relational evaluative flow). E-motion without Feeling is charge without direction. Feeling without E-motion is discernment without energy to act on it.

## III. Fire — Power — Kairos — Intuition

**Algebraic position**: P; the power variable; the rate of energy transfer.

**Elemental character**: generative, initiating, transformative. The quality of ignition. Fire is not heat — it is the process of transformation itself, the conversion of potential into kinetic, the moment when something that was possible becomes actual. Fire is always becoming rather than being.

**Functional character**: Intuition (Jungian) — the capacity to sense what is not yet present, to perceive the potential before it has manifested. Non-linear, synthetic, future-oriented. Intuition does not work sequentially; it arrives whole. It perceives the pattern before perceiving the components of the pattern. Fire/Intuition is an **arational** mode: it operates orthogonal to the evaluative axis, not prior to or beneath it. Intuition does not evaluate (that is Feeling/Water) and does not analyze (that is Thinking/Air) — it apprehends directly, by a different structural operation. The arational character of Fire is precisely what makes dual causation necessary: intuition (P-U, exteriorization) requires crystallization (I-R) not because it is incomplete but because it operates on a different axis whose complement must simultaneously be active for anything to come into form. See: *arational*, *dual causation* (L2-Terminology).

**Rhetorical character**: Kairos — the right moment, the opportune timing, the moment when something can be said or done that will be genuinely transformative. Kairos is not about what is said but about when and how. The same words at the wrong moment produce nothing; at the right moment, they change everything.

**PFOR position**: Potential — the source from which the system draws. Not potential in the sense of "not yet realized" but potential in the sense of energetic capacity, the ground of possibility.

**Tier**: Source (with Water/I). Fire and Water are the generative poles — the source tier — from which Air and Earth crystallize. Fire without Earth tends toward the Idol of the Theatre: grand transformative visions untethered from material grounding.

**Polarity**: Active. Fire moves outward, initiates, disturbs equilibrium.

**Shadow — Idol of the Theatre**: when Fire operates without Earth/Ethos grounding, it produces theatrical performance: the compelling vision that has lost contact with material reality. The grand theory that explains everything within its own terms but touches nothing it claims to describe. The charismatic leader who inspires but cannot deliver. The revolutionary who can see the new world but cannot build it.

**Relational character in the algebra**: P = UI — Power arises from the product of Voltage (Logos, potential difference, structural framework) and Current (Pathos, actual flow, relational connection). Kairos is not spontaneous; it requires both structural clarity and relational presence.

## IV. Water — Current — Pathos — Feeling

**Algebraic position**: I; the current variable; the rate of charge flow; the actual movement through the system.

**Elemental character**: flowing, connective, relational, evaluative. Water finds its level. Water takes the shape of whatever contains it while maintaining its own nature. Water is the medium of connection — it flows between and through, carrying what it carries from one place to another.

**Functional character**: Feeling (Jungian) — the evaluative function, the discernment of value. Not emotional charge (see E-motion distinction above) but the relational assessment of what matters, what fits, what is right. Feeling perceives value through connection: it knows the quality of a relationship before it can analyze why.

**Rhetorical character**: Pathos — the felt force that connects speaker and audience, the current of genuine relational presence. Not manipulation (which would be force without genuine connection) but the transmission of what is actually true about the situation through the medium of felt relationship.

**PFOR position**: Flow — the process by which the system moves, the actual dynamic rather than the potential.

**Tier**: Source (with Fire/P). Water is generative, but where Fire initiates, Water sustains and connects. The source tier generates what the manifesting tier (Air and Earth) structures and grounds.

**Polarity**: Passive. Water receives, responds, holds. Not passive in the sense of weak — passive in the sense of receptive, capable of being shaped while remaining itself.

**Shadow — Idol of the Cave**: when Water operates without Air/Logos structure, it produces the Cave: the world shaped entirely by personal emotional response, where individual or group feeling is mistaken for universal truth. The community that has become so relationally coherent internally that it cannot receive information that challenges its coherence. Certainty without the structure to test it.

**Relational character in the algebra**: I = U/R — Current arises from Voltage divided by Resistance. The relational flow is driven by potential difference (structural tension, the gap between where the system is and where it could be) and modulated by resistance (the grounding material through which the flow must pass). More structure creates more potential difference; more grounding directs the flow.

## V. Air — Voltage — Logos — Thinking

**Algebraic position**: U; the voltage variable; the potential difference that drives current flow.

**Elemental character**: clarifying, structuring, differentiating. Air creates the separation that makes potential difference possible. Where Fire transforms and Water connects, Air distinguishes — creates the conceptual space in which differences can be perceived and relationships can be articulated. Air is the medium through which Light travels: ideas require Air to propagate.

**Functional character**: Thinking (Jungian) — the analytical function, the capacity to build frameworks, derive logical consequences, and articulate structure. Thinking operates on distinctions: it creates and maintains the conceptual vocabulary through which the world is made legible.

**Rhetorical character**: Logos — rational argument, structured discourse, the explicit articulation of the reasons and relationships that make a claim intelligible. Logos is the face that Western intellectual culture has most thoroughly privileged and most thoroughly plane-locked into.

**PFOR position**: Opposition — the potential difference, the structural tension. Not opposition as conflict but opposition as the differential that drives movement. Without potential difference, there is no current flow; without structural tension, there is no process.

**Tier**: Manifesting (with Earth/R). Air and Earth crystallize from the Source tier's generative energy. Air provides the structural form; Earth provides the material ground.

**Polarity**: Active (with Fire). Air moves outward like Fire, but where Fire transforms, Air differentiates and structures. The active poles initiate; the passive poles (Water and Earth) receive and integrate.

**Shadow — Idol of the Marketplace**: when Air operates without Water/Pathos connection, it produces the Marketplace: language circulating without connection to the living realities it nominally describes. Words debating words. Frameworks describing frameworks. The map mistaken for the territory. The academic discourse that has lost contact with what it is about.

**Relational character in the algebra**: U = IR — Voltage arises from the product of Current (relational flow) and Resistance (grounded material opposition). The structural potential difference is generated by the interaction of relational movement and material constraint. Structure that has no current flowing through it and no grounding holding it generates no potential difference; it is structure in name only.

## VI. Earth — Resistance — Ethos — Sensation

**Algebraic position**: R; the resistance variable; the material opposition to current flow that grounds and directs.

**Elemental character**: grounding, stabilizing, materializing. Earth does not transform (Fire), flow (Water), or differentiate (Air) — Earth holds. Earth is what provides the material substrate through which the other three operate. Without Earth, Fire has no fuel, Water has no banks, Air has no medium to propagate through.

**Functional character**: Sensation (Jungian) — the direct perception of concrete physical reality. Not analysis of what is perceived (Thinking) or evaluation of its value (Feeling) or intuition of what could be (Intuition) — the direct apprehension of what is, here, now, materially present. Sensation is the face most directly in contact with the physical world. Earth/Sensation is an **arational** mode: it operates orthogonal to the evaluative axis, grounding the system in the concrete before evaluation or analysis is possible. Sensation does not judge or analyze — it registers, holds, and grounds. The arational character of Earth is precisely what makes crystallization (I-R) irreducible: felt value (Water/I) cannot become stable material pattern without the direct contact with physical reality that only Sensation provides. No amount of Feeling or Thinking substitutes for Earth's grounding function. See: *arational* (L2-Terminology).

**Rhetorical character**: Ethos — the speaker's credibility arising from demonstrated coherence between what they say and how they live. Not reputation as an external attribute but grounded character as an internal property made visible through sustained material engagement. Ethos is earned through contact with reality over time.

**PFOR position**: Result — what manifests, the concrete outcome. Not the potential (P), not the flow (I), not the structural framework (U) — the actual material consequence that has been produced through the interaction of the other three.

**Tier**: Manifesting (with Air/U). Earth provides the material ground that Air's structure requires to produce actual outcomes. Abstract structural frameworks (Air) without material grounding (Earth) produce Theatre.

**Polarity**: Passive (with Water). Earth receives, holds, grounds. The passive poles maintain and integrate what the active poles initiate.

**Shadow — Idol of the Tribe**: when Earth operates without Fire/Kairos transformation, it produces the Tribe: the species-level or culture-level habits of perception and categorization, mistaken for universal truth. The sense that our way of seeing things is simply how things are. The inability to recognize that the baseline from which we operate is a specific historically and biologically contingent formation, not the neutral ground.

**Relational character in the algebra**: R = U/I — Resistance arises from Voltage divided by Current. The grounding opposition is the ratio of structural potential to relational flow. High structure with low flow produces high resistance; high flow with high structure produces lower resistance. The material ground is not fixed; it is dynamically related to what flows through it and what structural framework shapes that flow.

## VII. The Polarity Structure

The four poles organize into two polarity pairs:

**Active poles** (Fire/P and Air/U): outward-moving, initiating, disturbing equilibrium. In electrical terms: the poles that generate or direct the driving force. In psychological terms: the poles that reach outward into possibility and structure.

**Passive poles** (Water/I and Earth/R): receptive, sustaining, maintaining. In electrical terms: the poles that carry and ground the flow. In psychological terms: the poles that receive, evaluate, and consolidate.

The polarity structure is not hierarchical. Neither active nor passive is superior; neither can operate healthily without the other. The active poles without the passive poles produce force without direction, structure without ground. The passive poles without the active poles produce stagnation without transformation, grounding without energy to ground.

The active/passive distinction maps to the Source/Manifesting tier distinction in a specific way: Fire (Source, Active) initiates; Water (Source, Passive) sustains; Air (Manifesting, Active) structures; Earth (Manifesting, Passive) grounds. The Source tier generates; the Manifesting tier crystallizes what the Source tier has generated.

A second axis, orthogonal to the active/passive axis, distinguishes the **rational** poles from the **arational** poles:

**Rational poles** (Air/U and Water/I): operating through evaluation and judgment. Air evaluates through structural analysis — building frameworks, deriving consequences, distinguishing true from false. Water evaluates through felt discernment — assessing value, quality, and relational fit. Both operate *on* what is presented to them, through the mediation of a judgment function.

**Arational poles** (Fire/P and Earth/R): operating outside the evaluative axis — not pre-rational, not against reason, but orthogonal to it. Fire apprehends directly through intuition — the pattern arrives whole, without sequential evaluation. Earth apprehends directly through sensation — the concrete reality registers immediately, before analysis. Neither Fire nor Earth evaluates in the sense of applying a judgment function; both operate by direct contact.

The rational/arational axis is not a hierarchy. Arational does not mean deficient or developmental; it names a structurally different mode of operation. The framework requires all four: rationality without arational grounding (Air and Water without Fire and Earth) loses contact with both concrete reality and transformative possibility; arational operation without rational structuring (Fire and Earth without Air and Water) produces charge and material presence without the evaluative structure that gives them direction and meaning.

## IX. The Higher-Dimensional Extensions

The WVG framework extends the four-pole DC structure into higher-dimensional registers through the concept of Elemental tiers. These were derived geometrically from the tetrahedron's spatial structure (WVG-Elemental-Worldview-Dimensions, 2025) before they were derived from circuit physics in the AC extension — the same structure shown twice, by geometry and by physics. The full geometric-to-physics derivation is in L3-K4-to-K5-via-AC-Extension Section II; this section gives the semantic content of each tier.

**5D — Quintessence/Essence** (ω in the AC extension): the temporal quality of the four-fold — not which configuration the system is in but *how fast it is navigating* through configurations. Geometrically, the centroid: dimensionless yet containing the whole volume, the condition of the four poles' coherence rather than a fifth pole beside them. This is the structural home of wisdom — the knowledge of *when* rather than *what*. The centroid's orthogonality to the realm's time-axis — and the threefold image it casts into manifest space — is developed in ProofL_Trinity.

**6D — Ground of Being/Embodiment**: the four-fold fully instantiated as a standing dynamic whole — all four poles, all six edges, all four faces present and mutually tensioned in a specific configuration at a specific moment. Where 5D is the centroid (the point), 6D is the tetrahedron held in tensegrity (the standing form). The register where the framework meets biography, ecology, and culture.

**7D — Purposeful Action/Manifestation**: the four-fold in coordinated service of a purpose beyond itself — the standing form of 6D now in trajectory, tracing a path through a larger context. The register where individual and collective telos become operative: not "what is this system's configuration?" but "what is it *for*, and where is it going?"

*Note on traversal ordering*: the zodiac's traversal order of the four poles and the unit circle's standard orientation are related by a coordinate transform (horizontal and vertical reflection). The structural mapping is sound; the directional conventions differ by a POV choice. Calculations that depend on traversal direction should specify which convention they use.

## X. Practical Orientation: Reading Any Situation

The framework's practical utility is in applying the four-fold as a coordinate system to any sufficiently complex situation. The application proceeds through the following questions, asked of the situation rather than derived from prior categorization:

1. **Where is the P/Fire/Kairos quality?** What is the generative charge here, the sense of possibility or urgency that indicates something significant is at stake? What is wanting to transform?

2. **Where is the I/Water/Pathos quality?** What is the relational current — the felt sense of connection, value, and evaluation? What does this situation *feel like* in the specific sense of what value and relationship it embodies?

3. **Where is the U/Air/Logos quality?** What is the structural framework — the articulated understanding, the explicit reasoning, the potential difference that drives movement? What is the conceptual scaffolding that gives this situation its shape?

4. **Where is the R/Earth/Ethos quality?** What is the grounding resistance — the material facts, the historical and biographical context, the demonstrated character and credibility of the participants? What has been shown to be true through sustained engagement?

The AbsentVar question: *which of these four is being held but not calculated?* Which face is present in the situation but not currently active — present as context, as the unspoken background, as what everyone knows but no one is addressing? The AbsentVar is often where the most important work is.

The shadow question: *which Idol is operating here?* Theatre, Cave, Marketplace, or Tribe — which pathological form of single-face operation is generating the situation's difficulties?

These questions are not a method to be applied mechanically. They are a way of orienting attention — bringing the four-fold into active contact with the situation, holding all four simultaneously (`.behold()`), before committing to a diagnosis or response (`.observe()`).

### Worked Example: The Holographic Extrusion (Reading the Interior)

Because the 12 equations of the DC algebra govern the internal matrix of a *single* system in complete mutual determination, the external poles and internal poles are mathematically bound. 

The **Source tier**—Fire/$P$ (internal drive/urgency) and Water/$I$ (relational current/feeling)—is subjective and invisible to the outside observer. 
The **Manifesting tier**—Air/$U$ (external structure/words/framework) and Earth/$R$ (material facts/physical choices)—is objective and highly visible. 

Because the system demands topological closure, the invisible interior ($P$ and $I$) is continuously extruded into the visible exterior ($U$ and $R$). They do not just correlate statistically across large populations; they are structurally bound at the level of the individual instance. 

If you observe a consulting professor, you cannot directly see their internal drive ($P$) or their relational empathy ($I$). But you can observe their structural framework ($U$—the rigidity of their logic, the organization of their speech) and their material grounding ($R$—their choice of transport, their physical posture, their material footprint). 

To a trained observer, these are not random traits. If you accurately map the professor's $U$ and $R$, the structural logic dictates the configuration of their $P$ and $I$. For example: an individual exhibiting massive, rapid-fire, inflexible theoretical frameworks (massive $U$) combined with a frictionless, highly-abstracted physical lifestyle (low $R$) structurally betrays a very specific internal configuration. By $I = U/R$, the relational current is erratic or ungrounded; by $P = U^2/R$, the internal drive is a compounding, unchecked engine of ambition masking a flight from the ground.

*A necessary constraint:* This method holds structurally, but the observer must not mistake the map for the territory. The calculation provides the topological shape of the character, but the specific, lived humanity of the instance can still surprise the observer. The equations diagnose the structural health of the system; they do not replace the requirement for genuine relational contact.

### Worked Example: The Gut Feeling

A concrete demonstration of the braid running in phenomenal experience — all four poles active in sequence, showing that what appears to be a single "irrational impulse" is actually a distributed four-fold process:

You are about to accept a job offer when a **gut feeling** stops you.

1. **Earth/Sensation registers first**: something in the concrete details feels wrong — the office environment, the interviewer's micro-expressions, the physical layout of the space. Sensation has registered a material fact before analysis has named it. (R active; P-U and I-R held as AbsentVar context)

2. **Fire/Intuition fires from that registered fact**: a pattern emerges whole — "this place has a hidden culture problem." Intuition does not derive this; it arrives with it. The pattern was implicit in what Sensation registered; Intuition makes it explicit as a gestalt. (P active; the Source-to-Manifesting edges now co-active — both exteriorization and crystallization running)

3. **Water/Feeling evaluates the pattern's significance**: not "is this true?" (that would be Thinking) but "does this matter to me, given what I value?" Feeling assesses the relational significance of what Intuition has named. "Yes — this conflicts with what I need in a working environment." (I active; evaluation of value, not analysis of structure)

4. **Air/Thinking is finally drawn in**: to articulate what has been found and decide what to do with it. "The culture problem is specifically about hierarchy — I can see it in X, Y, Z. My options are: decline, accept with eyes open, or ask a direct question before deciding." (U active; the structural framework that makes the decision legible and communicable)

5. **Integration**: the response that emerges from all four simultaneously is neither pure impulse nor pure analysis. It is grounded (Earth), pattern-complete (Fire), value-assessed (Water), and structurally articulated (Air). This is what "trusting your gut" actually means when it works: not bypassing reason but running the full braid before reason's output can be separated from its context.

The dual causation observation: steps 2 and 3 co-occur — Intuition exteriorizes (P→U, the pattern becoming nameable) simultaneously with Feeling crystallizing (I→R, the felt value becoming a stable assessment). Neither can run without the other. This is dual causation in phenomenal experience, not abstract structure.
