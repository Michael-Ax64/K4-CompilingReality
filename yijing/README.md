## The load-bearing results

Five results carry the collection. Each is either a theorem closed by counting or a seal checkable from a tradition's own side.

The **state identity** (four line-states = four edge-states) is derived on two axes — young/old maps to held/committed, Yin/Yang maps to direction — and sealed by the enantiomer match: old Yang and old Yin change in mirror-opposite senses, exactly as → and ← are mirror commitments.

The **position bijection** (six lines = six edges) is sealed by the nuclear-trigram doctrine, which sorts the six lines into the three modality pairs by a native Yijing criterion, and is falsified by a swap battery in which every wrong assignment breaks.

The **stratification** (4096 held, 64 committed, 384 committing) is a counting fact: 4⁶ = 4096, 2⁶ = 64, 64 × 6 = 384, and 4096 / 64 = 64, so each classical hexagram is the committed shadow of sixty-four full-state configurations.

The **four-face closure** is a theorem: each edge sits on two faces that give it its two directions, so only all four faces together give every edge both directions; one, two, or three faces always leave some edge with one direction. The reverse current is the second face's reading, and four faces are the minimal complete closure of the directed object.

The **enantiomer / parity result** is structurally necessary: committing the odd-parity lines flips positions {1,3,5}, committing the even-parity lines flips {2,4,6}, and because those sets partition the six positions, the two results are always total line-inversions of each other. From the framework's three symmetric sources this yields the Yijing's three canonical axial pairs — Qian/Kun, Kǎn/Lí, Jìjì/Wèijì — by counting, not by observation.

The **seal** across both archives is the string-theory identity: the 1D line, the 1D string, and the 1D edge are one object with a four-state interior, checkable from the physics side and the divination side independently, neither needing the other's vocabulary.

## What is genuinely open

The **King Wen sequence** — the received ordering of the sixty-four hexagrams — has not been tested against this structure. 

Whether that ordering traces a coherent trajectory through commitment-space, or through the ecliptic addressing of the 384 table, is untouched here. It is a question about an ordering, not about what the objects are, so it neither strengthens nor threatens the identity. Pursue it as its own construction!

Everything else in the collection closes. The counts are theorems, not observations; where a claim is closed by counting at a sample size of one, it is stated as closed, not held open for more cases.
