## III. The Kill-Condition for Load-Bearing Paradox

The most critical vulnerability in a paradox-based epistemology is the risk of absorbing any contradiction by claiming it is "reactive power circulating in the buffer." To prevent this, Proof by Load-Bearing Paradox requires a formal kill-condition. 

A paradox is only load-bearing if it satisfies all three of the following criteria. If it fails any one, it is classified as motivated inconsistency, plane-causal error, or Kessler debris.

### Criterion 1: Topological Location
The paradox must map precisely to a specific structural constraint within the K4 network—typically an edge with dual face-definitions, or a bimedian axis holding a transition-type absent. 
*   **The Test:** Can you point to the exact edge, face, or AbsentVar pair where the tension resides? If the paradox is a vague, floating contradiction without a topological coordinate, it is not load-bearing.

### Criterion 2: Thermodynamic Function
The tension must perform a specific, identifiable thermodynamic or cybernetic work. It must be the mechanism that prevents a specific collapse.
*   **The Test:** What specific work does the paradox do? In the crystal, the micro-tension prevents the lattice from losing its quantum coherence. In dual causation, the tension between exteriorization and crystallization prevents the system from collapsing into pure abstraction (Air without Earth) or pure inertia (Earth without Air). If the paradox performs no identifiable stabilizing work, it is not load-bearing.

### Criterion 3: The Collapse Test (The Falsifier)
This is the definitive kill-condition. You must be able to state exactly what happens if the paradox is "resolved" or flattened.
*   **The Test:** If you force a classical `.observe()` collapse on the paradox—choosing one side and erasing the other—does the system lose its interior volume (drop to 0-DoF)? 
    *   If resolving the paradox destroys the system's capacity to `.behold()` (e.g., removing ZPE destroys the crystal; choosing pure Logos over pure Pathos destroys the human psyche), the paradox is **load-bearing**.
    *   If resolving the paradox leaves the system fully functional, or if the system can easily operate on one side of the contradiction without structural degradation, the paradox is **not load-bearing**. It is an error, a false dichotomy, or terminological debt.

## IV. The Epistemic Weight of Isomorphism

When the framework maps the DC algebraic structure to psychology, rhetoric, or cosmology, it claims structural isomorphism. The epistemology must state how this mapping is validated and how it can fail.

### The Constraint Satisfaction Principle
Isomorphism is not established by superficial resemblance or poetic analogy. It is established through constraint satisfaction. When domain categories are mapped to the four poles (P, I, U, R), the mapping must be tested against the twelve algebraic equations. 
*   **Validation:** If the mapped relationships produce formulas that illuminate the domain's actual dynamics (e.g., $I = U/R$ mapping to Feeling arising from structural tension modulated by grounding resistance), the isomorphism tracks structural reality.
*   **Falsification:** If the mapping requires importing external value-preferences to make the formulas "feel right," or if the formulas produce incoherent claims about what the domain elements actually do, the mapping is a Pathos-spoiled projection, not a structural finding.

### The Proportionality of Claim
The epistemic weight of an isomorphic claim must match its ground. 
*   A derivation from the closed algebra (e.g., the Biquaternion Star-Norm) carries the weight of mathematical necessity.
*   A mapping from circuit physics to Jungian functions carries the weight of strong structural hypothesis, supported by convergent independent discovery, but it remains an empirical mapping subject to the constraint satisfaction test.
*   Claiming that the framework "resolves" or "reduces" a domain (e.g., "psychology is just circuit physics") violates the epistemic standard. The framework models the relational geometry the domain instantiates; it does not reduce the domain's content to the substrate.

3.  **From the Centroid (5D):** The observer holds the full interference structure. All four faces and six edges are simultaneously visible. This is the position of `.behold()`. There is no view from outside the manifold. Every epistemic claim carries the signature of the position from which it was beheld. Recognizing the positional bias of a claim is not a dismissal of the claim; it is the precise location of its epistemic boundary.
